Glance.

▪ THE BRIEF
A small screen that sits on your desk and pairs with your phone over short range. It mirrors notifications and calls so you can put the phone away and stop being distracted by it—but never miss something that matters.

The phone is the distraction. Don't replace it—put it away.

Most "focus" tools fight you on your phone, on the same surface that’s pulling you away. Glance moves the signal off the phone entirely: leave it face-down in a drawer, and the desk gets a quiet mirror of only the things you’d actually want to know.

No social feeds. No web. No general-purpose UI. A small ambient display, and that’s the whole product.

§ 01 · Overview What it is, and why it matters

The problem

Every focus app I’ve tried lives on the phone. Asking the phone to be its own bouncer is asking too much—the moment you unlock to dismiss a notification, the dopamine machine is open for business.

The thing that actually works is putting the phone in another room. The thing that stops us doing that is the fear of missing a call from a parent, a partner, a kid’s school.

The fix

Glance is a single-purpose desk display. Pair it once. Phone goes away. The display surfaces only what you’ve told it to surface—starred contacts, calendar, a small allow-list of apps. No tap, no scroll, no app store.

It’s a signal, not a screen. You read it the way you read a clock.

§ 02 · Architecture Phone → Glance · all on-device, all local
▪ SOURCE

Companion app

iOS / Android background helper. Reads the OS notification stream, applies the user’s allow-list, forwards filtered events.

→
▪ LINK

BLE tether

Bluetooth Low Energy GATT service. Encrypted, paired-once, ~10 m range. No cloud, no account, no data leaves the desk.

→
▪ SURFACE

Glance display

ESP32-S3 + 3.5" IPS panel. Render queue, dim/wake by ambient sensor, tap-to-dismiss only.

§ 03 · Design rationale What I'm holding firm on

No buttons except dismiss

Every input mechanism is a foothold for feature creep. Tap-to-dismiss is the entire control surface. If you want to act on a notification, the right place is your phone—go pick it up.

BLE only, no Wi-Fi

Wi-Fi means a setup wizard, an account, and an attack surface. BLE pair-once means I can ship without a cloud, and the data path is provably local.

Allow-list, not deny-list

Defaults matter. Users will not curate a deny-list; they will curate a tiny allow-list of 5–10 contacts and 2–3 apps. The product points everyone at that path.

An object, not a gadget

Industrial design has to read as desk object, not tech. Closer to a small clock than a tablet. That’s the next phase.
